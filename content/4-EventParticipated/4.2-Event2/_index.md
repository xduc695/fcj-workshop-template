# "FCJ Community Day - Stop Procrastinating, Start Building" Report

### Purpose of the Event

- Orient an effective and cost-free cloud computing (AWS) self-study roadmap.
- Share practical experience and the process of building products from major Hackathon competitions.
- Guide methods for psychological management, overcoming fear, and building confidence for technology students.
- Introduce Serverless architecture solutions, Managed Services, and optimal GenAI pipeline integration for Startups.
- Analyze a sustainable DevOps foundation and the application of systems thinking in project lifecycle management.

### List of Speakers

- **Hoang Thai Linh** - Presented the topic **“Level Up Your AWS Skills with Cloud Quest and Floci”**, focusing on learning methods through gamification and the open-source tool Floci.
- **Nguyen Tran Minh Quan, An Khuong Huynh, Quoc Anh Mai** - Shared practical experiences at *LotusHacks 2026*, the process of building two cloud projects *SynthHunter* (AI voice verification) and *Vortex* (recruitment optimization).
- **Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu** - Presented the topic **“Why We Always Need Confidence”**, focusing on psychology, the cost of self-doubt, and how to build confidence for tech students.
- **Tran Nghia** - Shared his startup story with the application **“Tu Vi Dai Viet”**, focusing on the process of migrating traditional infrastructure to the AWS cloud and the 3-layer AI processing architecture.
- **Tran Minh Quan** - Presented the topic **“The Hidden Iceberg of a Project: DevOps Before Disaster”**, analyzing hidden system errors from the perspective of systems thinking and the core foundation of DevOps.
- **Pham Khac Uy** - Presented the topic **“The Iceberg of Procrastination”**, focusing on deciphering the psychological root of procrastination and conveying the action philosophy of an AWS Builder.

### Highlights

#### Next-generation AWS learning method through Gamification

- **Reality for beginners**: Always pressured by costs, worried about bills exceeding the Free Tier limit, and incidents of forgetting to delete resources.
- **AWS Cloud Quest**: A free role-playing game practice platform, with detailed step-by-step instructions (Learn $\rightarrow$ Plan $\rightarrow$ Practice $\rightarrow$ DIY) helping learners easily hunt for prestigious *Digital Badges*.
- **Self-study optimization**: Seamlessly combining Cloud Quest and the open-source tool Floci to improve practical cloud skills.

#### Practical experience and Technology Pipeline at Hackathons

- **Hackathon specialty**: The extreme 36-hour race at *LotusHacks 2026*, the short time pressure (24-48h) to build an MVP product solving real-world problems.
- **SynthHunter Project (AI Voice Verification System)**: AI Deepfake voice detection architecture using *XLS-R (Speech Dynamics)*, *Whisper (Encoder Behavior)*, and *Pause Analysis (Temporal Rhythm)* models running an automated pipeline on AWS (S3, Lambda, EC2, Bedrock).
- **Vortex Project**: A closed-loop workflow optimizing the candidate recruitment journey from CV screening to behavioral and technical interviews.
- **Resource management**: Strategy to gather and thoroughly utilize free credits from major sponsors (AWS, OpenAI, Perplexity...) to optimize operational costs during the competition.

#### Psychology of Confidence and Procrastination in student life

- **Reality of barriers**: Students are technically good but anxious when presenting, leading to underestimation of their true abilities.
- **Redefining confidence**: *True Confidence* is not a big ego or perfection, but the willingness to try even when feeling anxious.
- **The iceberg of procrastination**: The outward manifestation is "laziness" (scrolling social media, turning off alarms), but the hidden part underneath (70-80%) includes hidden fears: *Fear of not being good enough (Imposter Syndrome), Fear of judgment*, and *Fear of failure*.
- **The guilt loop**: Avoiding difficult tasks $\rightarrow$ short-term comfort $\rightarrow$ approaching deadlines leading to guilt and self-blame $\rightarrow$ increased stress creating greater procrastination pressure.

#### Migrating to Serverless architecture and Managed Services for Startups

- **Startup reality**: Traditional models have high costs, difficult data retrieval, and complex terminology barriers for young users.
- **Tu Vi Dai Viet Architecture**: Migrating the entire system from traditional servers (Next.js, Nginx, PM2) to Serverless/Managed architecture on AWS (AWS Amplify, ECS, RDS PostgreSQL, ElastiCache) to optimize costs and increase load-bearing capacity.
- **In-depth 3-layer AI System**: 
  - *Layer 2 (Knowledge)*: Knowledge base integrating OpenAI embeddings model creating hashed 3072-d vectors, filtered by a Hybrid retriever.
  - *Layer 3 (Brain)*: Coordinated via Context assembler $\rightarrow$ running OpenAI LLM clusters in parallel $\rightarrow$ quality control with Output validator and optimized via Result cache.

#### Sustainable DevOps Foundation and Systems Thinking

- **Common misconception**: Thinking DevOps is merely installing and configuring technology tools (Docker, Kubernetes, CI/CD, Terraform).
- **Core pillars**: The DevOps Foundation must be solidly built from the trio: **People $\rightarrow$ Process $\rightarrow$ Technology**. You can automate the deployment process, but you cannot automate trust, communication, or a sense of responsibility.
- **Systems thinking**: Instead of traditional thinking that deals with fragmented issues and superficial symptoms (missed deadlines, system crashes), we need to look deep underneath to understand why the system repeatedly creates errors.

### What Was Learned

#### Design Thinking and Self-Management

- **Action comes first, confidence follows**: Confidence is not something you are born with; it is something you build and accumulate through action.
- **Breaking procrastination**: Applying the *5-second Rule* to seize opportunities before the brain finds reasons to procrastinate, combined with the *5-minute Rule* committing to do the first small task to create automatic momentum.
- **Culture of innovation**: Imbibing the AWS Builder philosophy: **GO BUILD** – Willing to fail fast to learn faster, continuously improving and delivering products.

#### Technical Architecture and Operations

- **Gamification model**: Understanding how to apply game structure to designing learning paths, making them less dry and more interactive.
- **GenAI Pipeline Optimization**: Learning how to design AI systems with output quality control (Validator) and memory caching (Cache) to ensure accuracy while optimizing token costs.
- **Modern cloud architecture**: Grasping the flow and load distribution of the system through API Gateway, AWS Lambda, Amazon Cognito, Amazon Bedrock, and S3/RDS storage clusters.
- **Practical DevOps mindset**: Understanding the importance of reducing friction, limiting human error through workflow automation (CI/CD, Infrastructure as Code), and breaking down silos between teams.

### Application to Work

- **Refactoring application architecture**: Researching and piloting the migration of background tasks and cron jobs of the current project to Serverless architecture with AWS Lambda and CloudWatch.
- **Practicing breaking procrastination**: Thoroughly applying the 5-minute Rule and 5-second Rule into the daily workflow to increase coding efficiency and cloud service learning.
- **Improving teamwork processes**: Promoting a culture of shared responsibility and smooth communication according to the core spirit of the DevOps Foundation in the current team project.

### Experience during the event

Participating in the **First Cloud AI Journey (Event 2)** was an extremely practical and inspiring experience, bringing a multi-dimensional perspective from in-depth technicalities to personal mindset orientation. Some outstanding experiences:

#### Learning from relatable practical stories
- The content was not purely theoretical but drawn from the 36-hour "surviving" experience at the Hackathon, hardware crashes, backaches, or sleep crises, full of authenticity.
- Listening to the journey of building a commercial Startup from scratch, helping to understand the practical problem when optimizing cloud infrastructure to save operational costs.

#### Approaching scientific psychological training methods
- Sharing sessions on confidence and imposter syndrome hit the psychological timidity and fear of crowds of the vast majority of technology students.
- Illustrating barriers with intuitive models like the Dunning-Kruger Effect helped me position where I am in the learning process so as not to get discouraged when facing difficulties.

#### Exploring modern architectural models
- Approaching a detailed automated AI pipeline system architecture diagram on AWS, from user security authentication, CDN load distribution to large AI model inference processing via Amazon Bedrock.
- Learning smart infrastructure transformation thinking and how to separate system components according to the iceberg model of DevOps.

#### Connecting and spreading the Builder spirit
- The event created a comfortable, humorous open space through pressure-relieving activities like free dancing (*Just Dance*) before zero hour, spreading a strong message: *"Don't be scared"* – don't be afraid, just step forward.
- Being introduced and connected to the next major technology playgrounds like the *Agentic AI Build Week* competition to continue the journey of practicing practical skills.

#### Lessons learned
- Solid technical expertise is not enough; without confidence and the ability to take responsibility, products are unlikely to reach customers and judges.
- Procrastination is a defensive feeling of fear; the only way to conquer it is to break the fear with the smallest actions immediately.
- The core foundation of every successful project always starts with understanding people and coordination processes; technology tools are just means to support automation.
