This process not only helped me transition from an on-premise mindset to a cloud-native approach but also trained me to work professionally, handle pressure, and solve real-world enterprise problems.

I successfully designed and deployed a **High-Concurrency Payment Gateway** architecture, implementing a secure 3-tier VPC network, containerization with **Docker & AWS ECS Fargate**, an isolated **RDS PostgreSQL** database, and setting up **CloudWatch/SNS** monitoring combined with encrypted backups using **S3 & KMS**.

I also attended community events to hone my skills in AI, Prompt Engineering, and DevOps career paths. ### Areas for Improvement
Although I have made significant progress, I acknowledge some realistic weaknesses that require serious attention and improvement:

* **Time Management and Discipline (Rating: Average):** Occasionally, I suffered from procrastination during the early weeks, which led to a pile-up of tasks over the weekends. I need to strictly apply the 5-Minute Rule and the Pomodoro technique to optimize daily productivity and avoid last-minute rushes.
* **Communication and Presentation Skills (Rating: Average):** When writing reports or explaining AWS workflows to beginners, I sometimes overused technical jargon, making it difficult for the audience to follow. I need to practice expressing complex ideas simply, using storytelling and visual diagrams instead of plain text.
* **Team Collaboration and Agile/Scrum (Rating: Average):** I spent the majority of the project researching and working independently. Therefore, I lacked hands-on experience in standard team collaboration workflows (e.g., Git branching, creating Pull Requests, peer code reviews, or resolving merge conflicts). I must proactively join larger team projects to develop this essential survival skill.
* **Expanding Infrastructure as Code (IaC) Skills:** Despite understanding system flows, my deployments were primarily manual (click-ops) via the AWS Console. This approach is highly prone to human errors and hard to replicate. My mandatory next goal is to translate this entire architecture into code using Terraform or AWS CloudFormation.
* **Advanced Technical English:** Although I can look up technical documentation, my reading speed and conversational reflexes in English meetings are still quite slow. This will be a major barrier when pitching ideas to international clients or working in multinational corporations.
